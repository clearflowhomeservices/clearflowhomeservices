Common causes of water pooling around a home

Most drainage problems start with one of three things: downspouts dumping water too close, grading that slopes toward the house, or soil that holds moisture. Over time, water can work into crawl spaces, basements, and even along the foundation wall.

  • Short or missing downspout extensions
  • Clogged gutters causing overflow and washout
  • Negative grading (yard slopes toward the home)
  • Low spots that collect water after rainfall
  • Improper drainage outlets / blocked discharge points

If you notice any of these, it‚Äôs worth getting a professional opinion before it becomes a bigger repair: standing water, erosion trenches, soil pulling away from the foundation, musty smells, damp crawl space, or water staining on foundation walls.

Typical drainage fixes (what a pro might recommend)

Fixes can be simple or involved depending on the cause. The goal is always the same: control water at the source, route it safely away from the structure, and prevent recurring saturation near the foundation.

  • Downspout extensions or underground downspout drains
  • Regrading / adding slope away from the home
  • Catch basins and yard drains for low spots
  • French drains and drainage lines for persistent pooling
  • Gutter adjustments to prevent overflow and splash-back
