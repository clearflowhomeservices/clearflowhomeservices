Why this matters

Water pooling can lead to foundation cracks and uneven settling. Erosion and washout can damage landscaping and expose the home‚Äôs perimeter. Moisture also increases mold and pest risk ‚Äî and the longer water sits near the home, the more expensive the solution becomes.

Common drainage problems homeowners run into

Most water issues start small: a short downspout, a low spot that collects water, gutters that overflow, or a yard that slopes toward the house. Over time, repeated saturation can weaken soil and push moisture where you don‚Äôt want it ‚Äî near the foundation and crawl space.

If you‚Äôre noticing the same puddles after every rain, it‚Äôs time to get it checked.

Common fixes a local pro might recommend

The goal is always the same: control water at the source, move it away from the home, and prevent repeat saturation.

Downspout extensions / buried downspout drains

One of the most common causes: roof runoff dumping at the foundation line. Extensions or underground piping can redirect water safely.

Regrading to slope away from the house

Negative grading funnels water toward the home. Regrading reshapes flow paths so runoff moves away from the foundation.

Catch basins, yard drains & low-spot drainage

For persistent puddles and low areas, basins and drains collect water and carry it to a proper discharge point.

French drains & drainage lines

When water consistently saturates an area, drains can relieve pressure and reduce pooling after repeated storms.

Gutter fixes to stop overflow & washout

Overflow and splash-back create erosion fast. Pros may recommend cleaning, pitch adjustments, guards, or larger downspouts.

Crawl space moisture control

If water is reaching the crawl space, exterior drainage is step one ‚Äî before encapsulation or dehumidification.
