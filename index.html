Why this matters

Water pooling can lead to foundation cracks and uneven settling. Erosion and soil washout can damage landscaping and expose the home‚Äôs perimeter. Moisture also increases mold and pest risk ‚Äî and the longer you ignore it, the more expensive it becomes after repeated rainfall.

Common fixes a local pro might recommend

The goal is always the same: control water at the source, move it away from the home, and prevent repeat saturation.

Downspout extensions / buried downspout drains

One of the most common causes: water dumping at the foundation line. Extensions or underground piping can redirect runoff safely.

Regrading to slope away from the house

Negative grading (yard sloping toward the home) funnels water straight to the foundation. Regrading reshapes flow paths.

Catch basins, yard drains & low-spot drainage

For persistent puddles and low areas, basins and drains collect water and carry it to a proper discharge point.

French drains & drainage lines

When water consistently saturates a zone, drains can relieve pressure and prevent pooling from returning after each storm.

Gutter fixes to stop overflow & washout

Clogs and overflow create trenches and erosion fast. A pro may recommend cleaning, guards, pitch adjustments, or larger downspouts.

Crawl space moisture control

If water is reaching the crawl space, addressing exterior drainage is step one‚Äîbefore encapsulation, dehumidification, or mold remediation.
